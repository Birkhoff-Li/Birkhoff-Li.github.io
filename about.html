About

Birk Li (李尤嘉) is originally from Qingdao, China — a port city perhaps best known internationally as the birthplace of Tsingtao Beer, the one already on the table at your local Chinese restaurant.

Left to his own devices in Vancouver by his parents, Birk developed an early and unhealthy passion for computer hardware and overclocking. In a moment of rebellion, he traded the Pacific coast for Montreal and enrolled at McGill University — a decision he has spent the better part of a decade pretending not to regret.

Those ten years at McGill's Macdonald Campus yielded a B.Eng., an M.Sc., and an ongoing PhD, all in Bioresource Engineering — which is the polite academic term for agricultural engineering. He is currently employed by Agricultural and Agri-Food Canada to develop the livestock component of DNDCver.Can, a Canadian agricultural ecosystem model.

About This Site

This site is inspired by the legendary simplicity of Hiroshi Abe's official website at abehiroshi.la.coocan.jp — widely celebrated on the internet for its radical, uncompromising plainness.

No frameworks. No JavaScript. No web fonts. Just HTML and a small CSS file.
