About

Birk Li (李尤嘉) is originally from Qingdao, China, a port city perhaps best known internationally as the birthplace of Tsingtao Beer, the one already on the table at your local Chinese restaurant.

Left in Vancouver with a generous trust fund and no parental supervision, Birk developed an early and unhealthy passion for computer overclocking. In a moment of rebellion, he traded the Pacific coast for Montreal and enrolled at McGill University, a decision he has spent the better part of a decade pretending not to regret.

Those ten years at McGill's Macdonald Campus yielded a B.Eng., an M.Sc., and an ongoing PhD, all in Bioresource Engineering (the polite academic term for agricultural engineering). He is currently employed by Agricultural and Agri-Food Canada to develop the livestock component of DNDCver.Can, an agricultural ecosystem model with a Canadian taste.

About This Site

This site is a tribute to the legendary personal website of Hiroshi Abe (阿部 寛), one of Japan's most acclaimed actors, known for the drama 下町火箭 (Shitamachi Rocket), which Birk watched religiously during his Vancouver days, and for winning the Japan Academy Prize for Best Actor.

His website, unchanged since the early 2000s, loads almost instantaneously: no scripts, no trackers, no images beyond a single headshot. It became Japan's go-to check: when the internet acts up, his page still loads. He is Japan's ping. His website is to the internet what DOOM is to embedded hardware.

This site borrows that same philosophy. Birk believes JavaScript is not a real language, and that the internet peaked when it was just plain HTML.
