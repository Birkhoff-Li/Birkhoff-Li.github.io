About

Birk Li (李尤嘉) is originally from Qingdao, China — a port city perhaps best known internationally as the birthplace of Tsingtao Beer, the one already on the table at your local Chinese restaurant.

Left in Vancouver with a generous trust fund and no parental supervision, Birk developed an early and unhealthy passion for computer overclocking. In a moment of rebellion, he traded the Pacific coast for Montreal and enrolled at McGill University — a decision he has spent the better part of a decade pretending not to regret.

Those ten years at McGill's Macdonald Campus yielded a B.Eng., an M.Sc., and an ongoing PhD, all in Bioresource Engineering — which is the polite academic term for agricultural engineering. He is currently employed by Agricultural and Agri-Food Canada to develop the livestock component of DNDCver.Can, an agricultural ecosystem model with a Canadian taste.

About This Site

This site is a tribute to the legendary personal website of Hiroshi Abe (阿部 寛), one of Japan's most acclaimed actors — known for the TV drama Trick, the film Always: Sunset on Third Street, and winning the Japan Academy Prize for Best Actor.

His website, unchanged in design since the early 2000s, has taken on a life of its own entirely separate from his acting career. Because it loads almost instantaneously — no scripts, no trackers, no images beyond a single headshot — it became an informal benchmark across Japan. When a new games console, router, or smartphone ships, there is a long-standing tradition of opening Hiroshi Abe's homepage as the first test of whether the internet actually works. He is, in a very real sense, Japan's ping.

This site borrows that same philosophy. No frameworks. No JavaScript. No web fonts. Birk believes JavaScript is not a real language, and that we should all go back to CLIs.
